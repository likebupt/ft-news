## Amazon
- **Real-world grounding in agentic AI** — Amazon describes four ways to better ground AI agents in operational settings so they behave more reliably and earn user trust. For finetuning/post-training, the key implication is that training data and evals should reflect real-world context, constraints, and feedback rather than only static benchmark tasks. [Read more](https://www.amazon.science/blog/real-world-grounding-in-agentic-ai)

- **Bridging intent and execution in agentic systems** — Amazon argues that the orchestration layer between models and tools is now a major bottleneck, and outlines simple harness-design principles to improve agent performance. This matters for finetuning because post-training quality depends heavily on the trajectories produced by those tool-use systems; better harnesses mean cleaner supervision and more reliable agent traces. [Read more](https://www.amazon.science/blog/bridging-intent-and-execution-in-agentic-systems)

- **Graviton5’s improved design increases speed and energy efficiency — beyond Moore’s law** — Amazon unveiled Graviton5 improvements, including chiplets, faster memory, and newer interconnects, claiming ~25% better performance for general-purpose and agentic AI workloads. For finetuning, this points to lower-cost infrastructure for running post-training jobs and serving fine-tuned/agentic models at scale. [Read more](https://www.amazon.science/blog/graviton5s-improved-design-increases-speed-and-energy-efficiency-beyond-moores-law)

## OpenAI
- **Supporting Europe’s work in ensuring a trustworthy AI ecosystem** — OpenAI said it supports the EU’s AI transparency and provenance efforts, including standards and tools to help identify AI-generated content. This is relevant to finetuning because compliance increasingly requires tracking synthetic data provenance, output labeling, and post-training safety/transparency measures. [Read more](https://openai.com/index/supporting-eu-trustworthy-ai-ecosystem)

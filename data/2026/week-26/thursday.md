# Final Weekly Digest

_All items this week are from academic/open research sources, so the digest is grouped by theme._

## Academic / Open Research

### Multimodal
- [**CoLA: Cross-Modal Low-rank Adaptation for Multimodal Downstream Tasks**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03314) — Extends LoRA-style adaptation to dual-stream multimodal models, aiming to improve downstream performance without full fine-tuning.
- [**Privacy-Aware Visual Language Models**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.17423) — Trains VLMs to recognize and handle privacy-sensitive visual content, relevant for multimodal safety and policy tuning.

### Reinforcement Learning / Online Adaptation
- [**ExTra: Exploratory Trajectory Optimization for Language Model Reinforcement Learning**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24994) — RL post-training method for verifiable rewards that targets poor exploration on both easy and hard prompts.
- [**Beyond One-Size-Fits-All: Diagnosis-Driven Online Reinforcement Learning with Offline Priors**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25527) — Suggests online RL systems should selectively use offline priors instead of applying them uniformly during adaptation.

### Agents / Evaluation
- [**The Interplay of Harness Design and Post-Training in LLM Agents**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25447) — Shows agent performance depends heavily on harness choices such as tool exposure, tool descriptions, and step-level context, potentially confounding evals.

### Safety / Alignment / Guardrails
- [**Do Thinking Tokens Help with Safety?**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25013) — Tests whether reasoning-style “thinking tokens” actually improve safety, rather than assuming they do.
- [**Do Encoders Suffice? A Systematic Comparison of Encoder and Decoder Safety Judges for LLM Adversarial Evaluation**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25782) — Compares encoder-based and decoder-based safety judges, with implications

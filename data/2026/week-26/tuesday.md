# Weekly Digest

## Open Research / arXiv

### Multilingual, Translation, and Language Control
- **CAT-Translate** — Compact Japanese↔English translation models make the case that specialized small models can still matter versus large multilingual systems, especially for efficient deployment. [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21413)
- **IndicGuard** — Multilingual safety guard model and dataset for Indic languages, addressing a major English-centric gap in alignment and moderation tooling. *(Link unavailable in provided summary.)*
- **First-Token Broadcasters** — Mechanistic study of why multilingual models drift into the wrong language; suggests targeted interventions may beat blunt full-model retraining for language control. [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22361)

### Post-Training, Alignment, and Agents
- **Post-Training Recipe, More Than Model Family, Shapes Multi-Agent LLM Conversational Behavior** — In multi-agent systems, conversation style may depend more on post-training choices than on the underlying base model family. [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20632)
- **GRPO Autoregressive Text-to-Image Post-Training** — Studies GRPO-style online RL for autoregressive text-to-image models, focusing on the tradeoff between preference gains and preserving output diversity. [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21498)
- **RL for Automated Code Compliance** — Uses RL to improve LLM systems that convert building regulations into machine-readable compliance rules, targeting precision in high-stakes workflows. [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22402)
- **PrivacyAlign** — Reframes privacy as a contextual alignment problem for agents, aiming to match model behavior to user-specific privacy preferences rather than fixed rules. [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21710)
- **Finetuning with Scientific Data Increases Hallucinations** — Finds that domain-specific scientific fine-tuning can worsen hallucinations, underscoring the need for explicit factuality checks in specialization pipelines. [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21359)

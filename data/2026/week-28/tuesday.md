## Final Weekly Digest

### Academia / arXiv
- **[Out-of-Distribution Generalization of Risk Aversion in Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02755)** — Tests whether risk-averse behavior learned during alignment transfers out of distribution, a key robustness question for safety and post-training.
- **[Safe Inference-Time Alignment via Lagrangian Reward Augmentation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02781)** — Proposes decoding-time steering of a frozen LM with auxiliary rewards, offering a cheaper alternative to repeated RLHF-style retraining.
- **[Reward Granularity in RLVR: Comparing Process and Outcome Reward Structures for Mathematical Reasoning in Small Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02869)** — Compares process rewards vs. outcome-only rewards in verifiable-reward RL for math reasoning.
- **[Spectral Rewiring for Exploration, Purification, and Model Merging](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03065)** — Introduces a post-training method aimed at efficient exploration, post-RL cleanup, and model merging without dense full-parameter updates.
- **Unbiased Alignment for Large Language Models with Noisy Preferences** — Focuses on reducing bias from noisy preference labels in RLHF/DPO-style alignment pipelines. *(Link not included in source summary.)*
- **[NormWorlds-CF: Solver-Verified Counterfactual Normative Reasoning with Metamorphic-Relation GRPO](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03957)** — Builds a solver-verified benchmark for normative reasoning and uses metamorphic-relation GRPO to reward correct reasoning, not just correct outcomes.
- **[Spinning Straw into Gold: Relabeling LLM Agent Trajectories in Hindsight for Successful Demonstrations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04235)** — Turns imperfect or failed agent rollouts into usable demonstrations, potentially lowering the cost of long-horizon agent post-training.
- **[dOPSD: On-Policy Self-Distillation for Diffusion Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04428)** — Extends post-training to diffusion LMs with on-policy self-distillation to improve reasoning in non-autoregressive generation.
- **Wrong Before Right: Late Rescue and Interface Failure in Aligned Language Models** — Finds “late rescue” and interface failures, suggesting some alignment errors come from output bottlenecks rather than missing internal knowledge. *(Link not included in source summary.)*
